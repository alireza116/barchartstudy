In this study, we will show you two types of bar chart and ask you to answer specific questions.
The first type is a standard bar chart, one with every bar corresponding to a value about a category. The taller the bar, the higher the value.
The second type is a modified version. We call it a wrapped bar chart. The way wrapped bar charts work is that bars taller than a certain threshold will wrap around themselves.
For example, a bar having a value of 5000 will wrap 5 times if the threshold is a 1000.
The horizontal parts of the wrapped bar charts do not count towards the total value of the bar. So you can simply estimate the total value of a wrapped bar by counting the number of times a bar has wrapped and multiplying by the threshold. The image below shows how it works.

In this study, we will ask you to use these two types of bar chart to answer questions about two datasets.

The questions we ask are of two types:
  • To find specific bars, such as the one with the highest and the lowest value]
  • And to compare the two bars in terms of ratio of highest to lowest.
For example, we will ask you how many times is the value of the tallest bar to the shortest bar.
For example, if a bar has a value of 5000 and another has a value of 500, the taller bar is 10 times the value of the shorter bar.
We are asking you to estimate, so please do your best.
